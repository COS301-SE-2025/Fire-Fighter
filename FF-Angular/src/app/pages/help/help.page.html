FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS

How long does FireFighter access last?

Firefighter access is time-bound and expires after the set amount of time when the request is made. Access is automatically revoked when the issue is resolved.

What happens if my request is denied?

Denied requests include detailed reasoning. You can appeal through the escalation process or contact the emergency support hotline for critical issues.

Can I extend my FireFighter session?

Extensions require additional approval and justification. Submit a new request with the original ticket reference and updated reasoning.

How are my actions audited?

All FireFighter activities are logged with timestamps, user attribution, and action details. Audit trails are immutable and reviewed regularly.

FireFighter Role Request Guide

Step-by-step instructions for creating emergency access requests

Follow these steps to create a FireFighter emergency access request. This process ensures proper documentation and approval workflow for elevated privileges.

1

Navigate to Requests Page

Go to the Requests page using the navigation menu. This is where you can view all your previous requests and create new ones.

2

Click the "+" Button

Look for the red "+" button on the requests page and click it to open the request creation modal.

3

Fill in Required Information

Complete all the required fields in the request form:

  • • Request Date: The date when you need the emergency access (automatically set to today)
  • • Emergency Type: Select from HR Emergency, Financial Emergency, Management Emergency, or Logistics Emergency
  • • Reason: Provide a detailed explanation of why you need emergency access (required field)
  • • Emergency Contact: Your contact number (can be auto-filled from your profile)
  • • Duration: Set the access duration between 15 minutes and 2 hours using the +/- buttons
4

Submit Your Request

Once all required fields are completed, click the "Submit Request" button. Your request will be sent for approval and you'll receive notifications about status updates.

Important Notes
  • • Emergency access requests require approval before activation
  • • Access is time-bound and expires after the set duration
  • • Provide detailed justification to expedite approval process
  • • You'll receive notifications about request status changes

Emergency Access Workflow

Complete workflow from request to ERP system access

This workflow guides you through the complete process of requesting emergency access in FireFighter and then using that access in the integrated ERP system to perform your required actions.

Phase 1: Request Emergency Access

Submit your emergency access request through FireFighter

1
Create Emergency Access Request

Navigate to the Requests page and click the "+" button to create a new emergency access request. Fill in all required information including emergency type, detailed reason, and access duration.

💡 Tip: Provide detailed justification to expedite the approval process
2
Wait for Approval

Your request will be reviewed by the appropriate approvers. You'll receive notifications about status changes. Monitor your notifications and the requests page for updates.

📧 Note: Email notifications will be sent for all status updates

Phase 2: Access Activation & ERP Usage

Use your approved access in the ERP system

3
Access Granted Notification

Once approved, you'll receive a notification that your emergency access has been activated. Your elevated privileges are now active in the integrated ERP system.

⏰ Time Sensitive: Your access timer starts immediately upon approval
4
Navigate to ERP System

Access the integrated ERP system using your normal login credentials. Your account now has the elevated privileges corresponding to your approved emergency type:

  • • HR Emergency: Access to HR module and user management
  • • Financial Emergency: Access to financial modules and reporting
  • • Management Emergency: Access to management dashboards and controls
  • • Logistics Emergency: Access to logistics and supply chain modules
5
Perform Required Actions

Complete your emergency tasks within the ERP system efficiently. All actions performed with elevated privileges are automatically logged for audit purposes.

📝 Audit Trail: All elevated actions are automatically tracked and recorded
6
Access Expiration

Your elevated access will automatically expire after the specified duration. You'll receive notifications before expiration and when access is revoked.

✅ Automatic Cleanup: No manual action required - access is automatically revoked
Best Practices
  • • Complete your emergency tasks as quickly as possible
  • • Monitor the remaining time on your access in the FireFighter dashboard
  • • Document any actions taken for future reference
  • • Log out of the ERP system when finished to ensure security
  • • Contact support if you need additional time before expiration

ERP Integration Management

Account linking and user group management

FireFighter integrates with your organization's ERP system to provide emergency access management. This integration requires proper account linking and user group configuration to function correctly.

User Permission Restrictions

Normal users do not have permission to manage their ERP User ID in FireFighter. This is a security measure to ensure proper account linking and prevent unauthorized access.

Account Linking Process

How to get your FireFighter and ERP accounts properly linked

1
Contact FireFighter Administrator

Reach out to your organization's FireFighter administrator to request ERP account linking. Provide them with your ERP username/ID and any relevant department information.

💡 Tip: Have your ERP username and department information ready when contacting the administrator
2
Administrator Links Accounts

The administrator will link your FireFighter account to your ERP user ID in the system. This creates the connection needed for emergency access management.

🔗 Secure Process: Only administrators can perform account linking for security reasons
3
User Group Assignment

The administrator will ensure you are added to the relevant user group in the ERP system based on your role and emergency access requirements:

  • • Financials Group: For financial emergency access
  • • HR Group: For human resources emergency access
  • • Logistics Group: For logistics and supply chain emergency access
  • • Manager Group: For management-level emergency access
4
Verification & Testing

Once the linking is complete, the administrator will verify that your account integration is working correctly. You can then begin using FireFighter to request emergency access.

✅ Ready to Use: Your FireFighter account is now properly integrated with the ERP system
Important Notes
  • • Account linking is a one-time setup process performed by administrators
  • • Your ERP username must match exactly for proper integration
  • • User group assignments determine which emergency access types you can request
  • • Contact your administrator if you experience any integration issues
  • • Changes to your ERP account may require re-linking by an administrator

Emergency Extensions

How to request additional time for emergency access

If you need additional time beyond your current emergency access duration, you can request an extension by creating a new request. This process ensures proper documentation and approval for extended access periods.

Extension Request Process

Follow these steps to request additional emergency access time

1
Create a New Request

Navigate to the Requests page and create a new emergency access request using the same emergency type as your current/previous request.

💡 Important: Use the same emergency type to maintain consistency with your original request
2
Update the Reason Field

In the reason field, provide an updated explanation that includes:

  • • Reference to your previous request (if applicable)
  • • Why additional time is needed
  • • Current progress on the emergency situation
  • • Estimated time needed to complete the work
3
Set Appropriate Duration

Choose the minimum duration needed to complete your emergency tasks. Remember that access duration is limited to between 15 minutes and 2 hours per request.

⏰ Timing Tip: Submit your extension request before your current access expires for seamless continuation
4
Submit and Wait for Approval

Submit your extension request and wait for approval. The request will go through the same approval process as your initial request.

📧 Notifications: You'll receive email and in-app notifications about the approval status
⚠️ Multiple Request Warning

If you make too many extension requests, all administrators will be automatically notified. Excessive requests may trigger an investigation into your emergency access usage patterns.

  • • Only request extensions when absolutely necessary
  • • Provide detailed justification for each extension request
  • • Plan your emergency tasks efficiently to minimize extension needs
  • • Frequent extension requests may result in access restrictions
Best Practices for Extensions
  • • Request extensions proactively, not at the last minute
  • • Be specific about why additional time is needed
  • • Estimate time requirements accurately to avoid multiple extensions
  • • Document your progress and remaining tasks clearly
  • • Consider if the emergency can be resolved through alternative means

Email Notifications

Types of email notifications and how to manage them

FireFighter sends automated email notifications to keep you informed about your emergency access requests and status changes. You can customize which notifications you receive through your settings.

Types of Email Notifications

Automated emails you'll receive during the emergency access lifecycle

Request Submitted

Confirmation email sent immediately when you submit a new emergency access request. Includes request details and tracking information.

📧 Contains: Request ID, emergency type, requested duration, and submission timestamp
Request Approved

Notification when your emergency access request has been approved and activated. Your elevated privileges are now available in the ERP system.

✅ Action Required: You can now access the ERP system with elevated privileges
Request Rejected

Notification when your emergency access request has been denied. Includes reason for rejection and guidance for resubmission if applicable.

❌ Contains: Rejection reason and recommendations for future requests
Access Expiring Soon

Warning email sent before your emergency access expires (typically 15 minutes before expiration). Allows time to request extensions if needed.

⏰ Timing: Sent 15 minutes before access expiration
Access Revoked

Confirmation when your emergency access has been automatically revoked due to expiration or manually terminated. Includes access duration summary.

🔒 Security: Confirms your elevated privileges have been removed
Managing Email Notifications

You can enable or disable email notifications for each type of event. Navigate to the Settings page and look for the "Delivery Methods" section to customize your preferences.

Go to Settings → Delivery Methods
Toggle email notifications on/off for each event type
Changes are saved automatically
Important Notes
  • • Email notifications are sent to your registered email address
  • • Critical notifications (like access expiration warnings) are recommended to keep enabled
  • • Email delivery may take a few minutes depending on your email provider
  • • Check your spam/junk folder if you don't receive expected notifications
  • • In-app notifications are always enabled regardless of email settings
